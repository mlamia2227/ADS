Android is an open source and Linux-based Operating System for mobile devices such as smartphones and tablet computers. Android is developed by the Open Handset Alliance, led by Google and other companies.

Android offers a unified approach to developing apps for mobile devices, which means that developers only need to develop for Android and their apps must be able to run on a variety of Android-powered devices.

The first beta version of the Android Software Development Kit (SDK) was released by Google in 2007, and the first commercial version, Android 1.0, was released in September 2008.

On June 27, 2012, at the Google I/O conference, Google announced the next version of Android, 4.1 Jelly Bean. Jelly Bean is an incremental update that aims to improve the user interface in terms of both functionality and performance.

iOS is a mobile operating system for devices manufactured by Apple. iOS works on iPhone, iPad, iPod Touch and Apple TV.

iOS is best known for serving as the main software that allows iPhone users to interact with their phones using gestures such as swiping, tapping, and pinching. These finger gestures are typically implemented on multitouch capacitive touchscreen displays that respond quickly and accept inputs from multiple fingers. Although not the number 1 mobile operating system globally, iOS dominates the North American market by a large margin, with a 60 percent market share as of May 2010.
